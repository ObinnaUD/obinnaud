About

I am a PhD candidate in Sustainable Finance at Africa Business School, University Mohammed VI Polytechnic in Morocco, where I conduct research on the intersection of climate science and financial economics.

My dissertation, "Essays on Climate Resilience, Climate Finance Impacts, and Market Mispricing in the Global South," examines how climate shocks transmit through economic systems and affect sovereign risk in developing economies. I employ methods including wavelet analysis, structural vector autoregression (SVAR), and Monte Carlo simulation to understand these complex dynamics.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I spent nearly three years as Regional Field Project Manager at OCP Africa in Nigeria, where I managed agricultural development projects and worked directly with farming communities to improve adoption of sustainable practices.

Research

Working Paper

Climate Shockwaves: Sovereign Risk Transmission Under Climate Stress

Using wavelet analysis to examine how climate-induced economic shocks transmit through sovereign bond markets in Global South economies. The paper investigates time-frequency dynamics of risk spillovers and identifies critical transmission channels.

Working Paper

Climate-Monetary Policy Interactions: Evidence from the UK

A structural vector autoregression (SVAR) analysis examining how climate shocks affect monetary policy effectiveness, with particular focus on sectoral transmission through food manufacturing versus agriculture channels.

In Progress

Rice Yield Variability and Climate Adaptation in Côte d'Ivoire

Analyzing regional variation in rice yields across three regions in Côte d'Ivoire (2012-2024), examining climate resilience strategies and their implications for food security policy.
